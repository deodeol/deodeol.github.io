Understanding the Heat Index

The heat index is a measure of how hot it feels when relative humidity is added to the actual air temperature. It is often referred to as the "apparent temperature" or "real feel" temperature. The heat index takes into account factors such as humidity, wind speed, and sunlight, which can all affect how our bodies perceive the temperature.
