Understanding El Nino in Indonesia

El Nino is characterized by a warming of the sea surface temperatures in the central and eastern equatorial Pacific Ocean. This warming disrupts the normal weather patterns, leading to changes in rainfall patterns and temperatures around the world. In Indonesia, El Nino events typically result in below-average rainfall, leading to droughts and water scarcity in many parts of the country. This can have severe impacts on agriculture, as crops fail due to lack of water, leading to food shortages and increased prices.
