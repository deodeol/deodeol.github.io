What is Declaration of Independence in AP Government?

Declaration of Independence in AP Government refers to the document that was adopted by the Second Continental Congress on July 4, 1776, declaring the thirteen American colonies as independent states and no longer under British rule. It is a founding document of the United States and outlines the principles and ideals upon which the nation was built.

History and Myth of Declaration of Independence in AP Government

The Declaration of Independence is often associated with the myth of a single momentous event where the document was signed in one sitting. However, the reality is that the declaration went through various drafts and revisions before its final adoption. It was a culmination of the colonists' grievances against British rule and their desire for self-governance.

Hidden Secrets of Declaration of Independence in AP Government

While the Declaration of Independence is a well-known document, there are some hidden secrets and lesser-known facts about it. For example, the famous phrase "all men are created equal" has been subject to interpretation and debate throughout history. Additionally, the declaration originally included a passage condemning the institution of slavery, but it was removed due to objections from southern states.

Question and Answer

Q: Why is the Declaration of Independence important in AP Government?

A: The Declaration of Independence is important in AP Government as it provides the foundational principles and ideals on which the American government is based. It outlines the rights of individuals, the purpose of government, and the concept of self-governance.

Q: Who wrote the Declaration of Independence?

A: The Declaration of Independence was primarily written by Thomas Jefferson, with input from a committee of five, which included John Adams, Benjamin Franklin, Roger Sherman, and Robert Livingston.

Q: What were the main grievances outlined in the Declaration of Independence?

A: The main grievances outlined in the Declaration of Independence included taxation without representation, the quartering of British troops, and the violation of colonists' rights to life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness.

Q: What impact did the Declaration of Independence have on the world?

A: The Declaration of Independence had a profound impact on the world by inspiring other nations to fight for their independence and adopt democratic principles. It served as a beacon of hope for people seeking freedom and self-determination.
