Texas Declaration of Independence Summary and Its Target

The Texas Declaration of Independence was adopted on March 2, 1836, by the delegates of the Convention of 1836, held at Washington-on-the-Brazos. It was a response to the increasing centralization of power by the Mexican government and the violation of the Mexican Constitution of 1824. The declaration states that the Mexican government had become a tyranny and that the people of Texas had the right to establish their own government.

What is the Texas Declaration of Independence?

The Texas Declaration of Independence is a document that formally declared the independence of Texas from Mexico. It outlined the grievances of the Texan people against the Mexican government and asserted their right to self-government. The declaration drew inspiration from the United States Declaration of Independence and other revolutionary documents of the time.

History and Myth of the Texas Declaration of Independence

The Texas Declaration of Independence was a pivotal moment in the Texas Revolution. It came after the Battle of the Alamo and the Goliad Massacre, events that galvanized the Texan people and strengthened their resolve for independence. The signing of the declaration marked the birth of the Republic of Texas, which lasted until Texas joined the United States in 1845.
