The History and Myth of Wimbledon Weather 14 Days

The weather has always played a significant role in the Wimbledon tournament. In fact, the tournament is famous for its "Wimbledon weather curse." This refers to the seemingly inevitable rain that occurs during the two weeks of the tournament. It is believed that the curse began in 1922 when the tournament was extended from one week to two weeks. Since then, rain has become a regular occurrence, causing delays and disruptions.

However, there is also a myth that suggests the Wimbledon weather curse is not a curse at all but rather a result of the tournament's positioning in the British summer. The theory is that the tournament coincides with the transition between the warm and cold air masses, leading to unstable weather conditions.
