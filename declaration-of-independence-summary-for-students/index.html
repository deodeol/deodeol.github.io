The History and Myth of Declaration of Independence Summary for Students

The Declaration of Independence was adopted by the Second Continental Congress on July 4, 1776. It was written by Thomas Jefferson, with input from the other members of the committee. The document was a formal declaration of the colonies' intention to separate from Great Britain and establish a new nation.

Over the years, the Declaration of Independence has taken on a mythical status in American history. It is often portrayed as a perfect and flawless document, representing the ideals and values of the United States. However, it is important to recognize that the Declaration of Independence was a product of its time and reflected the beliefs and values of the men who wrote it.

The Declaration of Independence was a revolutionary document, but it was not perfect. It did not address the issue of slavery, and it did not grant equal rights to women or Native Americans. However, it set forth a set of principles and ideals that would shape the future of the United States and inspire generations to come.
