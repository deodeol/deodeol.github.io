What is the Wimbledon Winners Board?

The Wimbledon Winners Board is a display that showcases the names of all the winners of the Wimbledon Championships since its inception in 1877. It is located at the All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club in Wimbledon, London. The board serves as a tribute to the champions who have graced the hallowed grounds of the Wimbledon courts and left their mark on the sport.

History and Myth of the Wimbledon Winners Board

The Wimbledon Winners Board has a rich history dating back over a century. It was first introduced in 1888, with the names of previous champions engraved on wooden panels. Over the years, the board has undergone several changes and upgrades, but its essence remains the same - to honor the champions and inspire future generations of tennis players.

There is also a myth surrounding the Wimbledon Winners Board. Legend has it that touching the board can bring good luck to players. Many aspiring champions make it a ritual to touch the names of past winners before stepping onto the Wimbledon courts, hoping to absorb some of their greatness and success.

The Hidden Secret of the Wimbledon Winners Board

One of the hidden secrets of the Wimbledon Winners Board is the meticulous process of adding a new name to the board. Each year, after the Wimbledon Championships, the winner's name is carefully engraved onto the board by highly skilled craftsmen. This process ensures that the board remains a timeless tribute to the champions of the past, present, and future.
