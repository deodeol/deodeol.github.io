What is the Nathan's Hotdogs Contest?

The Nathan's Hotdogs Contest is an annual competitive eating event held on the Fourth of July at Nathan's Famous restaurant in Coney Island, New York. It has been taking place since 1916, making it one of the longest-running and most iconic eating contests in the world. The contest consists of a 10-minute time limit, during which contestants must consume as many hotdogs and buns as they can. The current record stands at 75 hotdogs and buns, set by Joey Chestnut in 2020.

The History and Myth of the Nathan's Hotdogs Contest

The history of the Nathan's Hotdogs Contest is deeply intertwined with the history of Nathan's Famous restaurant itself. The contest was originally created as a way to settle a dispute between four immigrants on Independence Day. They each wanted to prove that they were the most patriotic, and what better way to do so than by seeing who could eat the most hotdogs? The contest quickly gained popularity and became an annual tradition at Nathan's Famous.
