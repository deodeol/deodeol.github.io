The history and myth of the 4th of July song meaning can be traced back to the early days of America. It was during the War of 1812, when Francis Scott Key wrote the poem that would later become the lyrics to "The Star-Spangled Banner." Inspired by the sight of the American flag still flying after a night of heavy bombardment, Key's words captured the resilience and determination of the American people.

Since then, "The Star-Spangled Banner" has become the national anthem of the United States, sung at sporting events, official ceremonies, and on the 4th of July. Its powerful lyrics and soaring melodies have made it a symbol of American pride and unity.
