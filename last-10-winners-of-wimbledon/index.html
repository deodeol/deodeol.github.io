The Wimbledon Championships, also known simply as Wimbledon, is the oldest tennis tournament in the world and is widely considered the most prestigious. Over the years, there have been many talented players who have won the championship. In this blog post, we will take a look at the last 10 winners of Wimbledon and explore their achievements and contributions to the sport.

Last 10 Winners of Wimbledon

The last 10 winners of Wimbledon include some of the biggest names in tennis and have left a lasting impact on the sport. These players have showcased incredible skill, determination, and sportsmanship on the grass courts of the All England Club. Let's dive into their journeys and celebrate their victories.

2011 - Novak Djokovic

Novak Djokovic, known for his powerful groundstrokes and defensive skills, claimed his first Wimbledon title in 2011. This victory marked the beginning of a dominant period for Djokovic, where he would go on to win multiple Grand Slam titles and establish himself as one of the greatest players of all time.

2012 - Roger Federer

Roger Federer, often regarded as the greatest tennis player of all time, secured his seventh Wimbledon title in 2012. Federer's elegant playing style and exceptional shot-making ability have made him a fan favorite throughout his career. His Wimbledon triumphs have solidified his status as a tennis legend.

2013 - Andy Murray

Andy Murray became the first British man to win Wimbledon in 77 years when he clinched the title in 2013. Murray's victory sparked a wave of national pride and cemented his place in British tennis history. His resilience and determination have inspired a new generation of players in the United Kingdom.

2014 - Novak Djokovic

Novak Djokovic claimed his second Wimbledon title in 2014, further establishing his dominance on the grass courts. Djokovic's ability to adapt to different playing conditions and his mental strength have been key factors in his success. His consistency and competitive spirit have set him apart from his peers.

2015 - Novak Djokovic

In 2015, Novak Djokovic added another Wimbledon title to his collection, showcasing his exceptional skills once again. Djokovic's relentless pursuit of success and his ability to perform under pressure have made him a formidable opponent on any surface.

2016 - Andy Murray

Andy Murray delighted the home crowd by winning his second Wimbledon title in 2016. Murray's never-give-up attitude and remarkable court coverage have made him a force to be reckoned with. His victories at Wimbledon have solidified his place among the tennis elite.

2017 - Roger Federer

Roger Federer continued to defy age and expectations by winning his eighth Wimbledon title in 2017. Federer's graceful playing style and sheer brilliance on the court have earned him a special place in the hearts of tennis fans worldwide. His longevity in the sport is a testament to his talent and dedication.

2018 - Novak Djokovic

Novak Djokovic made a triumphant comeback in 2018, securing his fourth Wimbledon title. Djokovic's mental fortitude and ability to rise to the occasion have been instrumental in his success. His fierce competitiveness and strategic gameplay make him a formidable opponent on any surface.

2019 - Novak Djokovic

Novak Djokovic claimed his fifth Wimbledon title in 2019, further solidifying his place among the tennis greats. Djokovic's exceptional athleticism and unwavering focus on the court have propelled him to numerous victories. His dedication to the sport and his relentless pursuit of excellence are truly commendable.

2021 - Novak Djokovic

In 2021, Novak Djokovic made history by winning his sixth Wimbledon title. Djokovic's incredible achievements at the All England Club have established him as one of the most successful players in Wimbledon history. His ability to perform under pressure and his unrivaled skill set make him a force to be reckoned with on the grass courts.

Conclusion

The last 10 winners of Wimbledon have left an indelible mark on the sport of tennis. Novak Djokovic, Roger Federer, and Andy Murray have displayed exceptional skills and determination, solidifying their places among the tennis greats. These players have not only entertained fans with their breathtaking performances but have also inspired a new generation of tennis players. As we eagerly await the next edition of Wimbledon, we can only imagine the incredible feats that will be achieved by future champions.
