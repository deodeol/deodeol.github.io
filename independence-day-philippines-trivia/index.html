History and Myth of Independence Day Philippines Trivia

The history of Independence Day in the Philippines dates back to June 12, 1898, when the country declared its independence from Spanish colonial rule. This declaration, known as the Proclamation of Independence, was made by General Emilio Aguinaldo in Kawit, Cavite. However, it wasn't until July 4, 1946, that the United States recognized the Philippines as an independent nation. In 1962, President Diosdado Macapagal changed the official date of Independence Day to June 12 to commemorate the country's true independence from foreign powers.

There are also several myths and legends surrounding Independence Day in the Philippines. One popular myth is the story of the Katipunan, a secret society that played a crucial role in the country's fight for independence. According to the legend, the members of the Katipunan used a secret code language called "Alibata" to communicate and plan their revolutionary activities. While the use of Alibata has been debunked by historians, the myth continues to capture the imagination of the Filipino people.

Hidden Secrets of Independence Day Philippines Trivia

Did you know that the Philippine flag plays an important role in the country's Independence Day celebrations? According to tradition, the flag should be displayed with the red stripe on top during times of peace and the blue stripe on top during times of war. Another hidden secret is the presence of the "Kawit Hymn" during the flag raising ceremony in Kawit, Cavite.
