History and Myth of Declaration of Independence Definition APUSH

The Declaration of Independence is one of the most important documents in American history. It was drafted by Thomas Jefferson and was heavily influenced by the ideas of Enlightenment philosophers such as John Locke. The declaration asserted the natural rights of individuals, including life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness, and declared that governments derive their power from the consent of the governed.

Over the years, the Declaration of Independence has taken on a mythical status in American culture. It is often seen as a symbol of American freedom and independence. However, it is important to recognize that the ideals expressed in the declaration were not fully realized at the time of its adoption, as slavery and other forms of oppression continued to exist in the newly-formed United States.
