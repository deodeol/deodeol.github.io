What are Scylla and Charybdis sisters?

Scylla and Charybdis are not biological sisters, but rather they are described as two separate sea monsters in Greek mythology. However, they are often mentioned together because of their close proximity and the dangers they pose to sailors. Scylla is a six-headed monster, while Charybdis is a whirlpool monster. They are said to reside on opposite sides of a narrow strait, and any ship passing through this treacherous area would have to navigate between the two monsters, risking destruction.

The History and Myth of Scylla and Charybdis

The story of Scylla and Charybdis dates back to ancient Greek mythology. According to one myth, they were once beautiful nymphs who caught the attention of the sea god Poseidon. However, Scylla rejected his advances, which infuriated him. As punishment, Poseidon transformed Scylla into a terrifying monster with six heads and twelve legs. Charybdis, on the other hand, was transformed into a whirlpool monster.

Another version of the myth suggests that Scylla was transformed by the witch Circe out of jealousy. Either way, the result was the same – Scylla and Charybdis became formidable monsters, forever doomed to guard the strait and terrorize sailors who dared to pass through.
