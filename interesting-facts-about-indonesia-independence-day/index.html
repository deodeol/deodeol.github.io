History and Myth of Indonesia Independence Day

The history of Indonesia's Independence Day can be traced back to August 17, 1945, when Indonesia's founding fathers declared the country's independence from Dutch colonial rule. This declaration, known as the Proclamation of Indonesian Independence, was made by Sukarno and Mohammad Hatta, who later became the first President and Vice President of Indonesia, respectively.

There are several myths surrounding Indonesia's Independence Day, one of which is the story of the flag-raising at the residence of Sukarno and Hatta. According to the myth, the flag used during the proclamation was sewn overnight by a group of Indonesian women, who worked tirelessly to complete it in time for the historic event.
