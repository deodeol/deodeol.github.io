History and Myth of Nathan's Hot Dog Contest

The Nathan's Hot Dog Contest has a rich history that dates back to the early 20th century. It all started in 1916 when four immigrants held a hot dog eating contest on the corner of Surf and Stillwell Avenues in Coney Island. The contest quickly gained popularity and became an annual event. Over the years, the competition grew in scale and attracted participants from all over the world. It has now become a Fourth of July tradition, drawing thousands of spectators and millions of viewers on television.
