What is an OMV NAS and How Does it Work?

An OMV NAS, or Open Media Vault Network Attached Storage, is a device that allows you to store and manage your files in one centralized location. It works by connecting to your local network and providing access to your files from any device connected to the network.

The OMV NAS device itself consists of one or more hard drives, as well as a network interface for connecting to your local network. Once connected, you can access your files using a web-based interface that allows you to browse, upload, download, and manage your files.

Setting up an OMV NAS is a straightforward process that involves connecting the device to your network, configuring the network settings, and setting up user accounts and permissions. Once set up, you can start uploading and managing your files using the web-based interface.

Overall, an OMV NAS is a powerful tool that allows you to store and manage your files in one centralized location, making it easy to access and manage your files from any device connected to your network.
