What is Charybdis?

In Greek mythology, Charybdis was a sea monster that lived in the Strait of Messina, a narrow passage between Italy and Sicily. It was described as a whirlpool that would suck in and destroy ships that came too close. Charybdis was often paired with another mythical creature, Scylla, who was a six-headed sea monster. Sailors had to navigate between these two dangers, hoping to avoid being caught by either.
