Scylla and Charybdis are two mythical creatures mentioned in Homer's epic poem, "The Odyssey." According to the legend, Scylla was a sea monster with six heads and twelve tentacle-like legs. Charybdis, on the other hand, was a whirlpool that swallowed and spewed out water three times a day. These creatures were positioned on opposite sides of a narrow strait, forcing ships to navigate between them, often resulting in disaster.
