What Protein is Present in CTE Brains?

Chronic Traumatic Encephalopathy (CTE) is a degenerative brain disease that is commonly found in athletes who have experienced repeated head injuries. It is characterized by the buildup of an abnormal protein called tau in the brain. This protein is associated with the development of various neurological symptoms and can have a profound impact on the lives of those affected.

Tau protein is a microtubule-associated protein that is normally found in the brain. Its primary function is to help stabilize microtubules, which are important for maintaining the structure and function of neurons. However, in cases of CTE, tau proteins become abnormally phosphorylated and clump together, forming tangles that disrupt normal brain function.

The presence of tau protein in CTE brains is a key characteristic of the disease. These abnormal tau tangles can spread throughout the brain, causing damage to neurons and leading to a variety of symptoms, including memory loss, confusion, mood swings, and cognitive decline. The severity of these symptoms can vary depending on the extent of the tau protein accumulation in the brain.

The Role of Tau Protein in CTE

To understand the impact of tau protein on CTE, it is important to delve into the underlying mechanisms of the disease. When an individual experiences a head injury, such as a concussion, it can trigger a cascade of events in the brain. One of these events is the release of tau proteins from damaged neurons. These released tau proteins can then become abnormally phosphorylated and form tangles, which can spread to other areas of the brain.

The accumulation of tau tangles in the brain leads to the dysfunction and death of neurons, which in turn contributes to the development of CTE symptoms. The exact mechanisms by which tau protein causes this damage are not fully understood, but research suggests that it disrupts neuronal communication, impairs cellular energy production, and triggers inflammation and oxidative stress.

History and Myth of Tau Protein in CTE Brains

The discovery of tau protein in CTE brains has revolutionized our understanding of the disease. Prior to this discovery, CTE was often misdiagnosed as other neurodegenerative disorders, such as Alzheimer's disease. The presence of tau protein tangles in the brains of athletes who had experienced repeated head injuries highlighted the unique nature of CTE and its association with trauma.
