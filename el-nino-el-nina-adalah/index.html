What is El Nino and La Nina?

El Nino and La Nina are two phases of the El Nino-Southern Oscillation (ENSO) cycle, which is a natural climate pattern that occurs in the tropical Pacific Ocean. El Nino refers to the warm phase of the cycle, while La Nina refers to the cool phase. These patterns are characterized by changes in sea surface temperatures, atmospheric pressure, and wind patterns in the Pacific Ocean.

The History and Myth of El Nino and La Nina

The phenomenon of El Nino was first recognized by fishermen off the coast of Peru in the 19th century. They noticed that every few years, the waters would warm, causing a decline in fish populations and changes in weather patterns. The term "El Nino" was used to describe the Christ child, as the phenomenon often occurred around Christmas time.

While El Nino and La Nina are natural climate patterns, human activities can also influence their intensity and frequency.
