The 4th of July, also known as Independence Day, commemorates the signing of the Declaration of Independence on July 4, 1776. This document declared the thirteen American colonies as a new nation, separate from British rule. The holiday has since become a symbol of freedom and patriotism in the United States.

There are many myths and legends surrounding the 4th of July. One popular myth is that Betsy Ross sewed the first American flag. While there is no concrete evidence to support this claim, it has become a widely accepted part of American folklore. Another myth is that fireworks were used to celebrate the first Independence Day. In reality, fireworks didn't become a popular tradition until many years later.
