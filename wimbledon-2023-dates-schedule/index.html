What is Wimbledon?

Wimbledon is the oldest tennis tournament in the world, and is widely regarded as the most prestigious. It has been held at the All England Club in Wimbledon, London since 1877. The tournament is known for its strict dress code, which requires players to wear all white. It is also known for its traditions, such as the playing of "The Star-Spangled Banner" before the start of each match.

Over the years, Wimbledon has become more than just a tennis tournament. It has become a cultural event, with celebrities and royalty often seen in the stands. The tournament is also known for its strict rules and regulations, which ensure that the integrity of the game is maintained.

History and Myth of Wimbledon

Wimbledon has a rich history that dates back over a century. The tournament was first held in 1877, and was originally open only to male players.
