What is Independence Day Rangoli 2020?

Independence Day Rangoli 2020 is an art form that involves creating intricate and colorful designs on the floor using colored powders, flowers, or rice. It is a popular tradition during festivals and special occasions, and Independence Day is no exception. The Rangoli designs often depict patriotic symbols and motifs, such as the Indian flag, the national bird, or famous freedom fighters. It is a way to express our love for our country and pay tribute to our nation's history and heroes.

Creating an Independence Day Rangoli 2020 requires skill and patience. The designs can be intricate and complex, and it is important to get the colors and proportions right. Traditionally, Rangoli was created using colored powders made from natural ingredients, such as turmeric, rice flour, and flower petals. However, nowadays, there are many ready-made powders available in the market that make the process easier and more convenient.

To create an Independence Day Rangoli 2020, start by cleaning the floor and making sure it is smooth and free from any dust or dirt. Then, sketch the design lightly using chalk or a pencil. Next, fill in the design with colored powders, flowers, or rice. You can use a small spoon or your fingers to carefully place the colors and create intricate patterns. Finally, add finishing touches, such as glitter or sequins, to make the design more vibrant and eye-catching.

History and Myth of Independence Day Rangoli 2020

The history of Rangoli can be traced back to ancient times. It is believed to have originated in the Indian subcontinent, and has been passed down through generations as a way to decorate homes and celebrate festivals. Rangoli is a Sanskrit word that means "colorful design", and it is often associated with good luck and prosperity. It is believed that creating a Rangoli at the entrance of a home can ward off evil spirits and bring happiness and prosperity to the household.

There are many myths and legends associated with Rangoli. One popular legend is the story of Lord Rama, the hero of the Hindu epic Ramayana. According to the legend, when Lord Rama returned to his kingdom after defeating the demon king Ravana, the people of Ayodhya welcomed him by creating beautiful Rangoli designs on the streets. They used colored powders, flowers, and rice to create intricate patterns and designs to celebrate his homecoming.

Another myth is the story of Goddess Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth and prosperity.
