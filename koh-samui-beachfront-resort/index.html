Koh Samui Beachfront Resort: A Rich History and Mythology

Koh Samui has a rich history and mythology that adds to its allure. According to legend, the island was formed when two giants, a brother and sister, threw rocks at each other from their respective homes in China and Myanmar. These rocks landed in the sea and became the islands of Koh Samui and Koh Phangan.

Throughout the years, Koh Samui has been influenced by various cultures, including Chinese, Indian, and Malay. This diverse heritage is reflected in the island's architecture, cuisine, and traditions. Exploring the local markets and temples, I was able to immerse myself in the vibrant culture of Koh Samui and gain a deeper appreciation for its history.

Koh Samui Beachfront Resort: Hidden Secrets

Koh Samui is home to many hidden secrets that are waiting to be discovered. One of these secrets is the Secret Buddha Garden, a mystical and enchanting place hidden in the hills of the island. The garden is filled with statues of deities, animals, and mythical creatures, creating a serene and otherworldly atmosphere.

Another hidden gem of Koh Samui is the Hin Ta and Hin Yai Rocks, also known as the Grandfather and Grandmother Rocks. These unique rock formations resemble male and female genitalia, and according to local legend, were created by an old couple who turned into rocks as a symbol of their everlasting love.
