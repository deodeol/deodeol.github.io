What is the Israeli Navy?

The Israeli Navy, also known as the Sea Corps, is the naval warfare service branch of the IDF. It is responsible for protecting Israel's maritime borders, conducting naval operations, and projecting power at sea. The Israeli Navy operates a wide range of vessels, submarines, and aircraft to fulfill its mission.

The history and myth surrounding the Israeli Navy are deeply intertwined with the establishment of the State of Israel. The Israeli Navy was officially founded in 1948, shortly after the declaration of independence. It played a crucial role in the Israeli War of Independence and has since evolved into a modern and technologically advanced naval force.

The Hidden Secrets of the Israeli Navy

One of the hidden secrets of the Israeli Navy is its expertise in underwater warfare. The Israeli Navy operates Dolphin-class submarines, which are equipped with advanced technology and capable of carrying out covert operations. Another hidden secret of the Israeli Navy is its close cooperation with other navies around the world. The Israeli Navy conducts joint exercises, exchange programs, and intelligence sharing with various countries to enhance its operational capabilities and strengthen international partnerships.
