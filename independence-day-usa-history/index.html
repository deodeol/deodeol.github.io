What is Independence Day USA History?

Independence Day USA History is the story of how the United States gained its independence from Great Britain. It is a tale of courage, determination, and the desire for self-governance. The American colonies were tired of being under British rule, and they fought for their freedom, leading to the birth of a new nation.

History and Myth of Independence Day USA History

The history of Independence Day USA is rooted in the American Revolution. The colonists, unhappy with British policies and taxation without representation, rebelled against the British Crown. The Revolutionary War began in 1775 and continued for several years. Finally, on July 4, 1776, the Second Continental Congress declared the thirteen colonies as independent states, free from British rule. This historic event is known as the signing of the Declaration of Independence.

Hidden Secrets of Independence Day USA History

While the history of Independence Day USA is well-known, there are some interesting hidden secrets associated with the holiday. One of these secrets is the fact that the Declaration of Independence was not signed on July 4th. The document was actually signed on August 2, 1776. Another secret is that John Adams, one of the Founding Fathers, believed that Independence Day should be celebrated on July 2nd, the day the Continental Congress voted in favor of independence.
