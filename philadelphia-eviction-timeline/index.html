The Philadelphia eviction timeline typically starts with a notice to vacate. This notice is usually given by the landlord or property owner and provides the tenant with a set amount of time to leave the property voluntarily. If the tenant does not comply with the notice to vacate, the landlord can then proceed with filing a complaint in court. Once the complaint is filed, a hearing will be scheduled, and both parties will have the opportunity to present their case. If the court rules in favor of the landlord, an eviction order will be issued. The tenant will then have a certain amount of time to move out before the eviction can take place.
