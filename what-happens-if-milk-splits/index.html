Answering the Question of What Happens If Milk Splits

When milk splits, it undergoes a process called curdling. This occurs when the proteins in the milk, specifically casein, coagulate and form clumps. The curdling of milk can be caused by several factors, including bacterial activity, changes in temperature, and the introduction of acids or enzymes. When these factors are present, the proteins in the milk bind together, causing the milk to separate into solids (curds) and liquids (whey). This process is more likely to occur in milk that is close to its expiration date or has been poorly stored.
