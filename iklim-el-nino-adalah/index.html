What is El Nino?

El Nino is a complex weather pattern that occurs when the waters of the Pacific Ocean warm up near the equator. This warming can disrupt normal weather patterns and lead to extreme weather events in various parts of the world. El Nino is typically associated with above-average sea surface temperatures in the central and eastern Pacific Ocean, along with changes in atmospheric pressure and wind patterns. These changes can cause droughts, floods, and other weather anomalies in affected regions.
